# How to Review Footage Before Editing

![Footage review framework](/blog/review-footage-before-editing/framework.webp)

If you review footage before editing, you save yourself from a very specific pain: discovering too late that the video is missing the shot that explains everything.

Editing is not where you want to find out that the audio is unusable, the close-up is soft, or the action does not connect.

By then the room is gone. The light is gone. Your energy is gone.

Footage review is the bridge between shooting and editing. It is not glamorous, but it is one of the most useful habits a creator can build.

## Review footage before editing with a checklist

Do not begin by dragging clips into a timeline.

Begin by watching.

Use five passes:

- Story pass: does the video make sense?
- Shot pass: do you have every required shot?
- Sound pass: is the voice clear?
- Continuity pass: do shots connect?
- Selects pass: which takes are usable?

This sounds slower than editing immediately. It is usually faster.

| Pass | Question | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Story | Does the footage prove the idea? | Missing or weak beats |
| Shot | Is every shot usable? | Selects and reshoot list |
| Sound | Can the viewer hear clearly? | Audio notes |
| Continuity | Do actions match? | Cut risks |
| Selects | Which clips are best? | Edit-ready bin |

Do this before you start making creative choices.

## The mistake: editing to hide missing thinking

![Good vs bad footage review](/blog/review-footage-before-editing/comparison.webp)

Many creators begin editing too early.

They cut fast because the footage feels weak. They add music because the pacing feels dead. They add captions because the explanation is unclear.

These tools can help.

But they should not be used to hide the fact that the video was not captured properly.

If the hook shot is weak, mark it. If the proof shot is missing, mark it. If the audio is bad, mark it.

The review should be honest because the edit depends on honesty.

A useful review sentence sounds like this:

"Shot 3 explains the fix, but the hand blocks the object. Reshoot close-up."

Not:

"Maybe we can make it work."

Sometimes you can. But you should know what you are compromising.

## Build a selects system

![Footage review process](/blog/review-footage-before-editing/process.webp)

A simple system is enough:

- A-roll: usable talking or main action.
- B-roll: supporting visuals.
- Inserts: close-ups, details, screens, hands.
- Audio: clean voice, room tone, sound effects.
- Reshoot: missing or broken.

Rename clips if needed.

You do not need a complex naming system. You need one you will actually use.

Example:

- `S01_hook_good`
- `S02_phone_close_soft`
- `S03_timer_best`
- `S04_payoff_reshoot`

Notice the note in the name.

It prevents you from opening the same bad clip ten times.

## Advanced section: professional review habits

![Professional footage review workflow](/blog/review-footage-before-editing/advanced.webp)

Professionals review for edit readiness.

They check:

- Is there enough pre-roll and post-roll?
- Are there clean audio starts?
- Do eyelines match?
- Does screen direction change randomly?
- Does the best performance have the best sound?
- Can the cut breathe?

For creators, the most important professional habit is checking sound first.

Bad visuals can sometimes be forgiven. Bad speech is harder. If the viewer must work to understand you, they leave.

Also check pacing before editing.

If every take starts with a long breath, note it. If the action takes too long, note where it should begin. If the best line is in the middle of a take, mark the timestamp.

Editing becomes easier when review creates decisions.

## Complete example: reviewing a Short

Short: "Fix your talking head lighting."

Footage:

- Hook talking head.
- Wide setup.
- Close-up of light.
- Before/after.
- Final rule.

Review:

| Shot | Status | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Hook | Usable | Best take starts at 00:03 |
| Wide setup | Usable | Background slightly bright, can grade |
| Close-up light | Reshoot | Hand blocks light position |
| Before/after | Strong | Use as proof shot |
| Final rule | Usable | Audio clean |

Reshoot only one clip.

This is the power of review. You do not vaguely feel that the video is weak. You know exactly what is missing.

## Final thought

Review is where the creator becomes the editor before opening the editor.

You are not polishing yet. You are deciding what the footage can honestly become.
