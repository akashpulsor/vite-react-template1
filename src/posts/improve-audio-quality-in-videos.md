# How to Improve Audio Quality in Videos

![Video audio quality framework](/blog/improve-audio-quality-in-videos/framework.webp)

To improve audio quality in videos, start with the voice. Not music. Not effects. Not plugins.

The voice.

For most creator videos, the viewer is there to understand a person. If the voice is thin, echoey, noisy, or buried under music, the video feels less trustworthy even when the visuals are good.

Creators often treat audio as something to fix later.

That is the expensive mistake.

Audio is easiest to improve before recording.

## Improve audio quality in videos by controlling distance

The fastest improvement is moving the microphone closer.

A cheap mic close to the mouth often beats an expensive mic across the room.

Sound follows distance brutally. Every extra foot adds more room, more echo, more noise, and less voice.

Use this simple priority:

| Priority | What to do | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Move mic closer | More voice, less room |
| 2 | Reduce echo | Softer room, clearer speech |
| 3 | Reduce noise | Fewer distractions |
| 4 | Level voice | Easier listening |
| 5 | Add music or foley | Support emotion, not clarity |

The order matters.

Do not add music to hide bad dialogue. It usually makes the problem worse.

## The common mistake: mixing everything at the same level

![Good vs bad audio mix](/blog/improve-audio-quality-in-videos/comparison.webp)

In a creator video, every sound has rank.

Dialogue is usually first.

Music is second or third.

Foley depends on the moment.

Ambience is last.

If all sounds fight at the same volume, the viewer has to work. The mix may feel energetic, but the message gets weaker.

Think of audio as layers:

- Dialogue: the main information.
- Room tone: the space.
- Music bed: emotion and pace.
- Foley: physical detail.
- Sync hits: emphasis.

Most of the time, the voice should sit clearly above everything else. Music should duck under speech. Foley should appear when it clarifies action, not constantly.

## A simple audio workflow

![Video audio process](/blog/improve-audio-quality-in-videos/process.webp)

Use this workflow:

- Record 5 seconds of room tone.
- Speak at normal energy.
- Keep the mic close.
- Avoid hard walls directly around the mic.
- Listen with headphones before recording all takes.
- Remove steady noise gently.
- Normalize loudness.
- Add music only after dialogue is clear.
- Add foley where the action needs weight.

Room tone is useful because silence in video is rarely silent. If you cut between clips with different background noise, the edit feels rough. Room tone helps smooth the gaps.

Foley is useful when the viewer needs to feel an action.

A timer click, pen scratch, shoe step, or light switch can make a Short feel more physical. But foley should be intentional. If every movement has a loud sound, the video feels fake.

## Advanced section: dialogue cleanup and music

![Professional audio workflow](/blog/improve-audio-quality-in-videos/advanced.webp)

Professionals separate dialogue cleanup from sound design.

Dialogue cleanup should preserve the voice. It can reduce noise, soften echo, level volume, and improve clarity. It should not change the speaker into someone else.

Sound design is different. It adds or shapes ambience, foley, music, and sync hits.

Do not confuse them.

If the speaker says a line, the cleanup should keep the words, timing, and texture intact. If you want music, generate or choose it separately. Then mix it under the dialogue.

A useful mix rule:

- Dialogue carries meaning.
- Foley carries physical action.
- Music carries emotional pace.
- Ambience carries place.

When in doubt, lower everything except the voice.

## Complete example: improving a phone video

Video: a creator explains a morning routine in a bedroom.

Problems:

- Voice sounds far.
- Fan noise is present.
- Music covers a few words.
- Jump cuts feel abrupt.

Fix:

| Issue | Action |
|---|---|
| Voice far | Use lav mic or place phone closer for audio |
| Fan noise | Turn off fan during take, record room tone |
| Music too loud | Duck music during speech |
| Abrupt cuts | Add room tone under edits |
| Weak action | Add subtle foley for timer click and notebook |

The final result does not need to sound like a movie.

It needs to sound easy to understand.

That is enough for most creator videos.

## Final thought

Audio quality is not mainly technical.

It is editorial. You decide what the viewer should hear first.
