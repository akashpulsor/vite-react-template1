# How to Plan a YouTube Short in 15 Minutes

![15 minute YouTube Short planning framework](/blog/how-to-plan-a-youtube-short/framework.webp)

If you search for how to plan a youtube short, you usually get advice that sounds reasonable but is hard to use: start with a hook, tell a story, keep it fast, add captions.

That is not wrong.

It is just not a plan.

A plan is something you can shoot from. It tells you what happens, where the camera is, what the viewer should feel, and what you must capture before you stop recording.

Most creators skip this because planning feels slower than shooting. So they open the camera, try a few takes, feel uncertain, record too much, and then hope the edit will save it.

Sometimes it does.

Usually the edit reveals the real problem: the short never had a point.

The purpose of a 15 minute plan is not to make the video perfect. It is to remove the obvious mistakes before they become footage.

## How to plan a YouTube Short without making it complicated

Use the 15 minute plan as a constraint.

You are not writing a film. You are making a small argument with pictures.

The short needs five things:

- A viewer with a specific problem.
- A hook that makes the problem visible.
- A sequence of beats.
- A shot list you can actually record.
- A sound or pacing note that keeps it alive.

Here is the clock.

| Minute | Decision | Output |
|---|---|---|
| 0-2 | Pick the viewer | One sentence about who cares |
| 2-5 | Write the hook | First line or first visual |
| 5-8 | Choose the promise | What the viewer will understand by the end |
| 8-12 | Break into beats | 3 to 5 moments |
| 12-15 | Write the shot list | What to record, in order |

This works because most Shorts do not fail from lack of effort.

They fail from vague intention.

For example, "morning routine" is not a plan. "A founder shows the 3 minute routine that stops him from checking his phone first thing" is closer. It has a viewer, a problem, and a tension.

The camera can now help.

## The common mistake: planning the topic instead of the change

![Good vs bad YouTube Short planning](/blog/how-to-plan-a-youtube-short/comparison.webp)

A topic is not enough.

"Productivity tips" is a topic.

"You are losing the first hour of your day because you let your phone choose your mood" is a change.

The viewer starts in one state and ends in another. That movement is the short.

Try this small framework:

- Before: what does the viewer currently believe?
- Friction: what makes that belief painful?
- Shift: what do you show that changes the belief?
- After: what should they do or notice next?

Example:

Before: "I need a long workout to feel awake."

Friction: "I keep skipping workouts because I do not have time."

Shift: "A 4 minute movement circuit can change energy quickly."

After: "Try the circuit before coffee tomorrow."

Now the video is not "fitness tips." It is a small proof.

This is why planning matters. It tells you what must be proven on camera.

## Turn the idea into beats

![YouTube Short planning process](/blog/how-to-plan-a-youtube-short/process.webp)

Beats are not shots. They are moments of meaning.

A 30 second Short usually needs 4 beats:

| Beat | Job | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Hook | Create tension | "I stopped using my phone for the first 10 minutes." |
| Setup | Show the situation | Phone on table, creator looking tired |
| Proof | Demonstrate the change | Timer, movement, quick routine |
| Payoff | Make the result clear | Creator focused, desk open, phone ignored |

Once you have beats, shots become easier.

Do not ask, "What should I film?"

Ask, "What does this beat need the viewer to see?"

If the beat is tension, show the phone lighting up. If the beat is proof, show the actual action. If the beat is payoff, show the changed state.

This prevents decorative footage. A beautiful shot that proves nothing is usually a tax on attention.

## Advanced section: how professionals compress planning

![Professional YouTube Short planning workflow](/blog/how-to-plan-a-youtube-short/advanced.webp)

Professionals do not plan every detail equally.

They identify the expensive mistakes.

For a YouTube Short, the expensive mistakes are:

- The hook does not create a question.
- The action is not visible.
- The shot order does not make sense.
- The audio is unclear.
- The ending does not resolve the promise.

A professional planning workflow looks like this:

- Write the hook as a visual and as a line.
- Decide the proof shot before any other shot.
- Build the rest of the short around that proof.
- Record sound notes before shooting.
- Review the first take against the plan.

The proof shot is the center.

If you are making a cooking Short, the proof shot may be the texture of the final food. If you are making a business Short, it may be a screen, a notebook, or a simple diagram. If you are making a fitness Short, it may be the movement form.

The proof shot answers the viewer's quiet question: "Is this real?"

## Complete example: a 15 minute plan

Topic: "How to stop procrastinating before work."

Viewer: someone who sits at a desk and avoids the first task.

Hook: "If you procrastinate before starting work, do this before opening your laptop."

Promise: the viewer will learn a tiny starting ritual.

Beats:

- Creator sits at desk, laptop closed, phone nearby.
- Creator writes one task on paper.
- Creator sets a 5 minute timer.
- Creator opens laptop only after the timer starts.
- Creator is working, phone face down.

Shot list:

| Shot | Camera | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Medium front | Creator frozen at desk |
| 2 | Close-up | Phone notification appears |
| 3 | Overhead | Hand writes one task |
| 4 | Close-up | Timer set to 5 minutes |
| 5 | Medium side | Laptop opens, creator starts |
| 6 | Close-up | Phone turned face down |

Sound notes:

- Keep voice clear.
- Use one soft click when the timer starts.
- Add a small room tone or low background bed.
- Do not let music cover the instruction.

Now you can shoot.

You may still improvise. But the improvisation is inside a structure. That is the useful kind.

## Final thought

A good Short is not a smaller long video.

It is a planned change in attention.

The viewer enters with one question and leaves with one useful answer.
